USAGE WARNING
Read before entering any seed words

This tool is for visually encoding or decoding an existing valid BIP39 seed phrase onto a Binary Grid dot map. It is not a seed generator.

DO NOT USE THIS PAGE TO GENERATE YOUR SEED PHRASE!

Creating a seed phrase here will result in insufficient entropy and an invalid checksum on the 12th or 24th word. Your funds would not be secure.

Always generate your seed phrase using a trusted, dedicated tool such as SeedSigner, a hardware wallet, or another audited offline generator.

SECURITY
Seed phrase & device safety
What this tool does

Offline BIP39 reference for stamping metal backup plates (KeyTag, TinySeed, NanoSeed). Displays your Compact SeedQR. Does not generate seeds, derive keys, show balances, or sign transactions.

NEVER SCAN OR PHOTOGRAPH THE SEED QR!
Never use on a connected device

Your seed phrase grants full, irreversible access to your funds. Even though this page makes no network requests, a connected OS can expose input via keyloggers, clipboard, screen capture, or malware.

ALWAYS USE THIS TOOL AIR-GAPPED!

Disconnect all networks (Wi-Fi, Ethernet, Bluetooth). Best practice: boot from a Linux live USB — leaves no trace. Wipe or power off after use.

Protect your seed phrase

Anyone with your full phrase can drain your funds instantly and permanently. Store only on durable physical media in multiple secure offline locations. Never photograph, digitise, or share it.

For significant value, consider a dedicated air-gapped machine or official hardware wallet recovery procedures.

HIGH SECURITY TIP
Passphrase protection

To avoid anyone who finds your punched metal backup gaining access to significant funds, seriously consider using a passphrase — also known as the 13th or 25th word.

STORE YOUR PASSPHRASE SEPARATELY FROM YOUR SEED!

Keep your passphrase only in your head or stored in a completely separate physical location from your seed backup. This means a thief who finds your metal plate cannot access your funds without also knowing the passphrase.

A passphrase is supported by all major hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor, Coldcard, Passport, Keystone, SeedSigner). It creates a completely separate wallet — make sure you test access before storing real funds.

INDEXING
1-based word numbering

DotBip uses a One Based Index system. This means:

abandon= 1
elephant= 575
zoo= 2048

Some apps use a zero based indexing system, but that is not a human way to deal with the words in the list. With zero-based indexing, abandon would be 0 and zoo would be 2047.

The original BIP39 word list has no numbers — they are added later by different software implementations, and this has caused confusion. A word's position on the list depends entirely on whether the developer started counting at 0 or 1.

STAY SAFE — DOCUMENT YOUR INDEXING SYSTEM!

Always record whether your backup was created with 1-based or 0-based indexing. When recovering, use the same system you used when encoding.

DEVICE GUIDELINES
High-security usage protocol

This standalone offline BIP39 tool (in the form of a single html file called dotbip-offline_vx.x.x.html) is for high-security minded Bitcoin users who physically stamp their 12- or 24-word seed into steel or titanium using the 12×12 or 12×24 Dot Map.

This tool is also designed to educate on the security aspects of the BIP39 standard and the Binary Grid encoding system.

SECURE USAGE PROTOCOL
1. Boot exclusively from a Linux live USB (Tails (tails.net) or a verified distro). Never use a persistent OS.
2. Disable and physically unplug all wireless interfaces — Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, and Ethernet — before booting.
3. We recommend using a laptop. No external mouse. No external keyboard. No extra USB devices. Malicious hardware exists that can silently log keystrokes or exfiltrate screenshots.
4. Avoid using this file on Windows, macOS, or any persistent OS. Spyware, keyloggers, and screen-grabbers are rampant on everyday operating systems.
Deviate at your own risk. Lost funds are irrecoverable.
